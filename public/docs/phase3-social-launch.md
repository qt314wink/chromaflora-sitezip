**Title:** "Six Colors and the Mathematics of Constraint"

**Format:** Solo, meditative-analytical

**Cold Open:**
"The most interesting creative decisions are the ones where you remove something rather than add it. Welcome to The Frequency. I'm Jennipher Troup. Today: six colors, zero backend, and what I learned about constraint as a creative principle."

**Act 1: The Constraint (0:30-3:00)**
"ChromaFlora is built on six hex codes. That's the entire color system. void, iris, aqua, bloom, ember, viridian. I chose six because I was trying to answer a question: how few elements do you need to express the full range of human emotion? Turns out the answer is close to six. Your eyes can distinguish millions of colors, but your brain uses about six emotional categories to process them: calm, energy, action, danger, warmth, growth. My six tokens map directly to those categories..."

**Act 2: The Discovery (3:00-7:00)**
"While building the step sequencer, I found the Bjorklund algorithm. It was invented in 1982 by a physicist named Bjorklund working on nuclear reactor fuel rods — specifically, how to evenly space them in the reactor core. The algorithm computes the most evenly-distributed pattern of k items in n slots. Mathematically perfect. A musicologist named Godfried Toussaint later applied this to rhythm analysis and discovered that virtually every 'feel-good' drum pattern across every culture that independently invented percussion music uses this exact distribution. The implication is staggering: there are rhythms that feel right because they're mathematically right. The system doesn't care what we call it. E(5,8) is a clave rhythm. It's also a nuclear physics solution."

**Act 3: Today's Discovery Card (7:00-10:00)**
"Today's discovery comes from the Acoustic Physics territory of the ChromaFlora engine. The card is about cymatics — the study of how sound creates visible geometric patterns in physical media. Sand on a plate, vibrating at exactly 432 Hz, spontaneously organizes into an 8-fold star pattern. At 528 Hz, it organizes into a 6-fold flower. These aren't coincidences. They're the eigenfrequencies of the medium. The question this card was answering: 'Does music have a visual form?' The answer is yes. And the form it takes is a mandala."
